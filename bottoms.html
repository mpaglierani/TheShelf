LETTERBOXD REVIEW

High school, cheerleaders, and a not-so-secret 'self-defense club'

Bottoms" (2023) struts onto the scene with all the subtlety of a bedazzled sledgehammer, and it's a wild ride through the high school landscape. Directed by Emma Seligman and starring Rachel Sennott and Ayo Edebiri, the film blends satirical comedy, teenage angst, and an unexpected fight club that's more about scoring dates than blows. It's a raucous mix of cheerleaders, chaos, and, of course, fashion faux pas.

PJ and Josie, played by Sennott and Edebiri, are the unapologetically awkward duo on a mission to score with the popular cheerleaders. Their plan? Create a feminist "self-defense club," but behind it all, there's a desire for something a bit steamier. When they accidentally bump the school quarterback with their car, rumors fly, and the chaos begins. With a lineup of quirky characters and wild twists, "Bottoms" takes the typical high school comedy and adds a dash of glitter, a sprinkle of rebellion, and a whole lot of pineapple juice.
