GOODREADS REVIEW

A Lrical Symphony of Love and War that Transcends Time

Madeline Miller's reimagining of the timeless Greek epic is a breathtaking odyssey that seamlessly blends the classical with the contemporary, crafting a narrative that is both achingly beautiful and profoundly resonant.

Miller's writing is lyrical, evocative, and steeped in the poetic traditions of ancient storytelling. She effortlessly transports readers to the world of gods and mortals, infusing each page with the vibrant energy of her narrative.

At the heart of this retelling is the tender and powerful love story between Achilles and Patroclus. Miller's portrayal of their relationship is a masterclass in character development and emotional nuance, transcending the bounds of time and mythology to create a love story that feels both epic and intimately human.

"The Song of Achilles" is a reclamation of voices silenced by centuries of storytelling. Miller breathes new life into characters like Patroclus, giving them agency and depth that adds layers of complexity to the familiar narrative.
