GOODREADS REVIEW

A Literary Dreamscape Where the Fantastical Meets the Visceral

Carmen Maria Machado's collection of short stories is a mesmerizing journey through the surreal and the sublime, where the boundaries between reality and fantasy blur into a haunting tapestry of narrative brilliance.

Machado's prose is a revelation, a literary ballet that dances gracefully between genres. From the speculative to the macabre, each story is a testament to her narrative dexterity, leaving readers spellbound and eager for the next unexpected twist.

The thematic richness of "Her Body and Other Parties" is staggering. Machado fearlessly tackles issues of identity, desire, and the female experience with an unapologetic and razor-sharp pen. Her exploration of the complexities of the body, both physical and metaphysical, is both profound and beautifully disquieting.

This collection is more than a book; it's a literary kaleidoscope that reflects the diversity and complexity of the human experience. Machado's narratives are a mirror held up to society, reflecting its beauty and its darkness in equal measure.
