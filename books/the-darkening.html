GOODREADS REVIEW

A Richly Woven World Full of Magic curses, Political Intrigue and Morally Gray Characters

The Darkening is set in an atmospheric world where a cursed city’s only hope lies with the daughter of failed revolutionaries and a Prince who fears the very throne he’ll inherit. Prince Dalca was born for one purpose: to protect his home from the deadly storm that surrounds his city and curses everyone it touches. Vesper Vale is the daughter of failed revolutionaries. Since the Queen sentenced her mother to death by the storm, she and her father have been on the run.

When the queen’s soldiers, led by Prince Dalca, catch up to them, Vesper will do whatever it takes to protect her father from sharing her mother’s fate. Even if it means arming herself with her father’s book of experimental magic, infiltrating the prince’s elite army of soldier-sorcerers or cheat her way into his cold heart.
