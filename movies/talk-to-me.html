LETTERBOXD REVIEW

A Gripping Directorial Debut

Talk to Me is an unnerving exploration of the supernatural that takes audiences on a twisted journey through the consequences of communication with the dead. The film begins with a shocking incident at a house party, setting the stage for a series of dark and supernatural events that unfold in unexpected ways.

The narrative centers around Mia, portrayed by a compelling lead, as she grapples with the trauma of her mother's suicide and becomes entangled in a chilling ritual involving a severed and embalmed hand. The film cleverly builds tension, keeping viewers on the edge of their seats as Mia and her friends delve deeper into the paranormal.

The cinematography effectively captures the eerie atmosphere, enhancing the film's overall sense of dread. The portrayal of possession is particularly chilling, with standout performances that convey the characters' struggles as they grapple with malevolent spirits.
