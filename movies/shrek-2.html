LETTERBOXD REVIEW

Where Happily Ever After Meets Happily Ever Laughter

Shrek 2 is not just a sequel; it's a comedy extravaganza that proves green is the new gold in the world of animated films. This delightful romp through the fairy-tale kingdom takes the irreverence of the original and cranks it up to an enchanting eleven.

The dynamic duo of Shrek and Donkey, voiced by Mike Myers and Eddie Murphy, returns with even more hilarious banter and antics. The addition of Puss in Boots, expertly voiced by Antonio Banderas, is a stroke of feline genius, stealing scenes and hearts with his swashbuckling charm.

The humor in Shrek 2 is as layered as an onion, appealing to both kids and grown-ups. From clever pop culture references to tongue-in-cheek jabs at fairy-tale clichés, the film keeps the laughs rolling. The inspired use of classic songs in unexpected ways adds a musical twist that's both nostalgic and side-splitting.

The animation is vibrant and full of whimsy, bringing the fairy-tale world to life with a modern twist. The character design, from the iconic trio to the new faces, is a perfect blend of endearing and hilariously exaggerated. The story, a charming mix of romance and family dynamics, keeps the pace lively and the audience engaged. It's a fairy tale with a Shrek-sized twist that subverts expectations while delivering heartwarming moments and belly laughs.
